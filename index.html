Charlie Parker

Songwriter, Saxophonist (1920–1955)


Charlie Parker image by William P. Gottlieb, Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons

Biography

Charlie Parker was a legendary Grammy Award–winning jazz saxophonist who, with Dizzy Gillespie, invented the musical style called bop or bebop. He was nicknamed "Bird" and "Yardbird". His associated acts include Dizzy Gillespie, Max Roach, and Miles Davis.

On March 12, 1955, at the age of 35, Parker died of pneumonia and the devastating effects of long-term substance abuse.

Three Essential Albums

Charlie Parker made numerous albums. These three are essential for any jazz music fans.

Album Title Year of Original Recording Notes
Yardbird Suite – The Ultimate Charlie Parker Collection (Rhino) 1946-1949 A great introduction to the music of Charlie Parker and the jazz/bop revolution.
The Complete Live Performances On Savoy (Savoy) 1947-1949 Prime recordings of Charlie Parker's early years.
The Complete Charlie Parker With Strings 1949 This album is known as Charlie Parker's personal favorite.
